Livelihoods and Access to Finance

Sector >> Livelihoods and Access to Finance

Poverty reduction and livelihoods improvement continues to be the most challenging agenda for human development due to the increasing divide between the well-off and the poor and the highly inequitable growth observed across geographies. While the correlation between the economic growth and poverty reduction is apparent, it is increasingly becoming evident that it is neither proportional nor uniform. Consequently, the livelihoods sector remains prominent and a priority area for development agencies.

We have extensively worked in the livelihoods and poverty reduction programmes since inception and have assisted our clients in programme design, strategy formulation for social inclusion of marginalized groups, need assessment, process design, monitoring and evaluation, social and legal reviews, designing communication strategy, capacity enhancement of institutions of poor and vulnerable groups, and improving community access to micro-credit and financial institutions.

Select Work in Livelihood and Access to Finance

Impact Evaluation and Monitoring for developing and implementing a comprehensive M& E Framework for tracking the progress of financial awareness interventions under IFC's Financial Awareness Project

2014 - 2016

Data Collection Poverty and social baseline assessment for PSIG MFI partners FOR Small Industrial Development Bank of India (SIDBI)

2016 - 2017

International Finance Corporation's Financial Awareness project was started in the year 2013 to pilot select financial awareness programs. The goal of this project was to expand the range of financial services available to low income households (from credit to other products like Insurance, Savings, Pensions, Remittances etc.) by increasing the demand for such services. Establishing a sustainable and replicable delivery model for financial awareness, incorporating financial awareness as a strategy amongst Financial Institutions, and empowering low income households through financial awareness training to take informed decisions in selecting financial products were the key interventions of this programme in order to widen the range of financial services.

Sutra was engaged as a monitoring and evaluation agency to develop and implement an Monitoring &Evaluation framework for each of the participating financial institution to monitor implementation of the financial awareness project at each participating Financial institution. Cost-benefit analysis of the financial awareness program at each participating Financial Institution, considering the cost incurred in delivering the financial awareness program and revenue generated thereof was another significant component. In addition our team was also responsible for evaluating different approaches of delivering financial awareness to assess change in financial position of the client using data captured by the participating Financial Institutions in their MIS. Our work resulted in developing a comprehensive Monitoring &Evaluation framework for the project intervention with indicators clearly identified for reporting costs incurred and revenue

The DFID supported Poorest States Inclusive Growth (PSIG) Programme in India aims to reduce poverty and foster economic growth by expanding access to financial services and promoting pro-poor investments in eight low income states. Sutra Consulting has conducted a poverty and social baseline assessment for PSIG MFI partners by SIDBI during July 2016 to May 2017. Sutra implemented and assessed poverty scorecard consisting of PPI indicators and other relevant questions for 30 microfinance institutions partners under the PSIG project and covering 21,000 HHs. The goal was to generate data that provides information on the similarities and differences between the state level and MFI level benchmarks on poverty outreach.

Sutra Consulting has provided its critical inputs in administration of poverty scorecard consisting of PPI indicators and other relevant questions for statistically representative sample of microfinance clients for about 30 microfinance institutions- partners under the PSIG project, enable efficient iteration of the questionnaire, Mapping of prescribed questionnaire in digital template, real time tracking of results, contributing in developing poverty focused social performance reports for MFIs.

Consultancy Services for Process Monitoring of National Rural Livelihood Project, Karnataka State Rural Livelihood Promotion Society (KSRLPS)

2016 - 2017

Process Monitoring of North East Rural Livelihood Project

2017 - 2018

Karnataka State Livelihood Project, named as Sanjeevini, is being implemented under Department of Rural Development and Panchayati Raj and aimed at improving livelihoods of rural poor and enhances social and economic empowerment of rural poor especially women. The programme is taking significant initiatives in improving income levels of the rural poor through interrelated components of social, financial and economic inclusion. It aims to form and strengthen community based institutions and improve access to financial and non-financial services.

Sutra Consulting provided monitoring support to the programme in five focussed districts where our team of advisers were responsible for developing evidence-based design of process monitoring framework and system, design of multi-stakeholder strategy, developing participatory monitoring systems and tools and developing capacities of the project to monitor and track progress of project outcomes and impacts. Our work in the programme has led to identifying operational and strategic changes needed to undertake midcourse correction for the project and provide inputs to bring institutional sustainability. The teams input has helped the programme in building community perception, providing feedback on satisfaction with project services reflecting on inclusion and institutional dynamics.

North East Rural Livelihood Project (NERLP) by the World Bank is implemented in the states of Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim and Tripura to assist the rural poor and vulnerable groups, including women, in improving their livelihood. The primary objective of the project is to empower the poor and vulnerable in rural areas directly so that they are able to take advantage of the opportunities for improvement of their livelihoods. The programme intends to bring the change through social empowerment focuses on empowering rural communities by creating sustainable community institutions, improving inclusiveness and ensuring equity in social and gender norms, Economic Empowerment and Partnership Development.

Sutra Consulting provided monitoring support to the programme in two focussed states of intervention, Tripura and Nagaland, where our team of advisers were responsible for developing evidence-based design of process monitoring framework and system, design of multi-stakeholder strategy, developing participatory monitoring systems and tools and developing capacities of the project to monitor and track progress of project outcomes and impacts. Our work in the programme has led to identifying operational and strategic changes needed to undertake midcourse correction for the project and provide inputs to bring institutional sustainability.
